Enjoy this travelogue, of sorts, of some of my favorite views from around the world. Shot on 35mm film + digital.

Sandstorms can wipe out your vision and slow your day for hours, forcing people inside home or inside cloth veils. Niger, 2012

The dark red sand of Southern Niger holds fertile promise for farmers, who can grow vegetables, grains, and fruits in abundance with the proper irrigation systems. Niger, 2012
Nigerien soldiers are happy to visit the Timia Oasis in Northern Niger. The town hasn't seen many tourists since al-Qaeda began operating in the Sahara, and they give a show of dancing and camel racing to welcome a visit from the Prime Minister and American delegation. The military has advanced the area before our arrival. Niger, 2012

Outside the Nigerien capital, Niamey, the land is sandy and rocky, with little vegetation or people. Niger, 2012

People gather in Northern Niger to welcome the Prime Minister and American Ambassador. They've prepared signs, dances, food, and a camel race. Niger, 2012
